MAPO TOFU

Tender tofu cooked in an umami and spicy sauce, accompanied by minced meat, Mapo tofu is one of the tastiest ways to prepare this healthy ingredient.

Mapo tofu (麻婆豆腐, ma po dou fu) is one of the most popular dishes from Sichuan cuisine. Sichuan is known for bold flavours and unapolegtic spices, mapo tofu being a key representative dish that is now known worldwide.

When I think of comfort foods, I think of the recipes that my mom and grandparents cooked while growing up; Mapo Tofu is one of the first recipes that come into mind. It is a savory and spicy dish that will have you downing bowls and bowls of rice.

INGREDIENTS

  • spring onions
  • soft tofu
  • pork mince
  • ginger
  • garlic
  • chili doubanjjang
  • soy sauce
  • cornstarch
  • sugar
  • chili powder
  • cooking oil

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a large non-stick pan or wok, fry garlic and ginger on medium heat with oil until lightly golden and aromatic.
  2. Add pork mince and brown on high heat, breaking the chunks apart with a spatula.
  3. Push the pork mixture to the rims of the pan, making room in the middle of the pan/wok. Add spicy doubanjiang and fry on medium heat to 'wake the spice' up, for about 1 minute. Add in chilli powder, sugar and Sichuan peppercorn (if using). Mix everything in the pan well.
  4. Add soy sauce and 1 cup of water. Mix well then gently add tofu cubes. Lift the pan/wok off the stove and gently swirl the pan to combine tofu with the pork sauce. Bring up to a boil then reduce heat to low and simmer (without a lid) for 3 - 5 minutes.
  5. To make the thickening mixture, combine cold water with cornstarch and mix well. Pour the thickening mixture into the pan and gently swirl the pan to thicken the sauce and coat the tofu.
  6. Sprinkle spring onions into the pan, simmer for a further 30 seconds then serve.
